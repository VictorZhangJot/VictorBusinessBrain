# MESSAGING HOUSE
## Victor · Jobs of Tomorrow

---

## FOUNDATION

**The Central Tension:**
Hiring managers in healthcare and AI/Data Centre know they need to fill a role — but they don't have the time, network, or current market knowledge to find someone genuinely qualified. They're stuck choosing between hiring fast and hiring right.

**The Unique Messaging Angle:**
Jobs of Tomorrow doesn't just fill the gap — it closes it with someone informed by daily-updated candidate data and AI-assisted screening, then negotiates a true win-win between budget and candidate expectations, instead of just closing the deal.

**Short (10–15 words):**
Recruitment for healthcare and AI/Data Centre roles, built on daily-updated candidates and a win-win close.

**Medium (25–35 words):**
Jobs of Tomorrow finds qualified healthcare and AI/Data Centre talent fast — using AI-assisted screening against a continuously refreshed candidate database, then negotiates outcomes that work for both the hiring manager's budget and the candidate's expectations.

## MESSAGING PILLARS

### Pillar 1: "Recruitment is not a transaction — it's a read on where your industry is going"
- Why it matters to the ideal client: Hiring managers don't want to just fill a seat; they want clarity on what their competitors might be hiring for and where the market is moving.
- Proof point: Victor's approach includes making recommendations based on current market trends, not just submitting resumes against a job description.
- In their words: "They will understand that recruitment agencies are not just transactional but instead we can see the full picture on the impact of the hiring."

### Pillar 2: "A win-win, or it's not a placement worth making"
- Why it matters: Hiring managers are wary of agencies that are commission-hungry and push placements through regardless of fit.
- Proof point: Victor explicitly factors in the hiring manager's budget and the candidate's expectations before closing — not just JOT's interest in closing the deal.
- In their words: "It will not be solely based on my company's interest in closing the deal."

### Pillar 3: "Your candidate pool should be as fresh as today, not three years old"
- Why it matters: Outdated skillset assumptions — especially in AI/Data Centre — lead to hiring for capabilities that no longer match where the industry is going.
- Proof point: JOT proactively scrapes and updates its candidate database daily, using AI to surface candidates who are actively looking right now.
- In their words: "We are able to utilize AI and scrape resumes from databases that are updated by the candidates on a daily basis."

### Pillar 4: "No agency fees taken from candidates — ever"
- Why it matters: Foreign healthcare workers are sometimes forced to pay commissions that cost them months of salary, building distrust of agencies before they even start.
- Proof point: Victor has explicitly stated JOT will not take fees from candidates, directly countering the industry stereotype.
- In their words: "We will not be taking fees from the candidates and I mentioned that it is not right."

## TOP 5 PROBLEMS & SOLUTIONS

**Problem 1:** "Headcounts need to be filled asap but I lack the resources and avenues to find the right talent."
- Their solution: JOT scopes requirements directly with the hiring manager, then screens its existing database plus AI-sourced candidates before any resume is sent.
- What makes it different: A proactive, daily-refreshed database means candidates are already active and reachable, not stale leads.
- The transformation: A shortlist within days instead of an open-ended search.

**Problem 2:** "Bringing in foreign healthcare workers means navigating source-country and local regulatory requirements I'm not well-versed in."
- Their solution: JOT manages the regulatory and pass-application process so the hiring manager doesn't have to.
- What makes it different: This removes an entire category of hiring-manager workload, not just the sourcing step.
- The transformation: Hiring managers no longer need to "spend time on getting the regulatory work, passes, and applications approved."

**Problem 3:** "I might be hiring for skillsets that were relevant 3–5 years ago, not what the AI/Data Centre space actually needs now."
- Their solution: JOT tracks current market trends and advises on what skillsets are actually in demand before the search even starts.
- What makes it different: Positioning as a market-informed consultant, not just an order-taker for a job description.
- The transformation: Hiring decisions that keep the company progressing instead of falling behind.

**Problem 4:** "I don't have time to evaluate whether recruitment agencies are genuinely invested in this hire or just trying to close a deal."
- Their solution: Regular, proactive updates and a willingness to flag when search outcomes aren't ideal, with suggestions to fine-tune the role or salary.
- What makes it different: Victor positions himself as "an extended arm of their company," not an outside vendor.
- The transformation: Hiring managers feel assured of follow-through, not left wondering what's happening behind the scenes.

**Problem 5:** "I'm worried that filling this role is just a stopgap, not something that actually helps my business grow."
- Their solution: JOT frames every placement around its business impact — citing the F&B operations manager story as the model outcome.
- What makes it different: The agency measures success by what the hire frees the business to do next, not just whether the seat is filled.
- The transformation: A hiring manager who, like the F&B founder in Victor's story, gains the bandwidth to focus on growth instead of day-to-day gaps.

## EMOTIONAL LANDSCAPE

### What drives them to act:
- Identity: "I want to be seen as a hiring manager who makes sharp, market-informed decisions."
- Achievement: "I want to finally fill this critical role with someone who actually stays."
- Relief: "I want to stop searching for a needle in a haystack with no time to spare."
- Connection: "I want to feel like I have a partner in this, not just a vendor sending resumes."

### What blocks them:
- Fear barrier: Hiring the wrong person again → underneath is a fear of being seen as ineffective by their own leadership.
- Resource barrier: No time or bandwidth to evaluate market trends themselves → the real issue is they need a consultant's judgment, not just more job board access.
- Belief barrier: A lingering industry stereotype that recruitment agencies are commission-driven and transactional.
- Timing barrier: The urgency of an open headcount makes it tempting to settle for "good enough" rather than wait for the right fit.

**The bridge:**
By treating every search as a market-informed consultation rather than a transaction, Victor moves hiring managers from "I just need this filled" to "I now understand my market better, and the right person is already shortlisted."

## BRAND VOICE GUIDELINES

**Voice attributes (3–5):**
- **Precise** — gets to the point without padding. Do: "Within a couple of days we sent three to five candidates." Don't: "We work tirelessly to bring you only the best."
- **Consultative** — frames hiring as a business decision, not a transaction. Do: "Here's what your competitors might be hiring for." Don't: "We have tons of great candidates for you."
- **Transparent** — flags when something isn't working rather than oversell. Do: "The search outcome wasn't ideal — let's fine-tune the role or salary." Don't: pretend every shortlist is perfect.
- **Fair-minded** — explicitly balances both sides of a placement. Do: "This works for your budget and the candidate's expectations." Don't: push a placement that only serves the deal closing.
- **Sincere** — no inflated claims about being unlike anyone else. Do: state what JOT actually does and why. Don't: rely on generic superlatives.

**Words to use:**
qualified, win-win, market trends, bottleneck, headcount, shortlist, screening, regulatory, replacement guarantee, extended arm, daily-updated, first-mover advantage

**Words to avoid:**
"best-in-class," "unparalleled," "synergy," "talent acquisition solutions," "we guarantee success," any language implying candidates are charged fees
