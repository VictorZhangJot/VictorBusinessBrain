Connecting top talent to AI and Data Centre companies · now
The hardest AI roles to fill have nothing to do with coding. (Save this + Repost if useful ♻️) The AI and data centre build-out is moving faster than most talent pipelines. Here is what is actually shifting in 2026: 1. Power and electrical engineers are the most contested hire in the market. Data centres consume electricity at city scale. The people who design and manage that infrastructure are disappearing into long-term contracts before most companies even post a job. 2. MLOps is splitting into two separate roles. Model deployment and model monitoring now require different skill sets. Companies posting one job for both are losing candidates to the ones that are not. 3. Thermal and cooling specialists have become urgent hires. GPU density creates heat loads most facilities were not designed for. The companies that missed this are now paying emergency contractor rates to fix it. 4. AI governance roles are mandatory in regulated industries. Banks, insurers, and healthcare firms are hiring compliance and safety specialists ahead of legislation. This is not optional for them. 5. Senior infrastructure talent supply is lagging demand. The gap is real and widening. The fastest-moving teams are hiring for aptitude and upskilling internally rather than waiting for the perfect CV. 6. Flexible arrangements are winning the talent war. Senior technical candidates in this space are turning down five-day office mandates. Firms still insisting are cycling through the same shortlist on repeat. Good luck. P.S.
