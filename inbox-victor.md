## SECTION 3: HOW DELIVERY WORKS

1. Victor meets with the hiring manager to understand the role's requirements, the purpose of the hire, and any challenges already faced in filling it.
2. He summarizes the requirements and begins screening through JOT's existing candidate database.
3. AI tools are used alongside the database to speed up candidate search and identify the right skillsets.
4. A first round of AI-assisted screening sifts out candidates who match the required skillset.
5. Only qualified, shortlisted candidates or resumes are sent to the hiring manager.
6. When a candidate is selected, Victor facilitates negotiation — balancing the hiring manager's budget against the candidate's expectations for a win-win outcome.
7. The 3-month replacement guarantee applies from the date of placement.

## SECTION 9: COMMUNICATION GUIDELINES
*(Practical application — not voice strategy)*

**Tone by context:**
- LinkedIn posts: Precise and insight-led — sharing market trends and job opportunities rather than generic recruiter content.
- Sales conversations: Consultative — understanding the purpose of the hire and challenges faced, not just collecting a job description.
- Client onboarding: Structured — requirement scoping first, regular updates throughout, transparent flagging if search outcomes aren't ideal.

**Language to use:**
qualified, win-win, market trends, bottleneck, shortlist, screening, extended arm, daily-updated, first-mover advantage, regulatory

**Language to avoid:**
Generic recruiter phrases, anything implying candidates are charged fees, anything that frames a placement as purely transactional.

**Hard limits:**
Victor has explicitly stated JOT will not take fees from candidates, directly opposing a common industry practice.
