# RULE OF 1
## Victor · Jobs of Tomorrow

---

## THE ONE FOCUS

- **One Avatar:** The Stretched Hiring Manager — an HR or hiring manager with an urgent headcount gap and no time to chase it down properly
- **One Problem:** "I need this role filled fast, but I don't have the network, time, or up-to-date market knowledge to find someone genuinely qualified."
- **One Solution:** Scope the real requirement → AI-assisted screening against a daily-updated database → win-win shortlist and negotiation
- **One Outcome:** The role is filled with someone who stays, and the hiring manager gains a clearer read on where their market is heading

---

## ONE AVATAR: The Stretched Hiring Manager

She's running day-to-day operations — or managing a team that is — and an open role has been sitting unfilled for too long. She's posted the job, maybe scrolled through a stack of resumes that don't quite fit, and she knows she doesn't have the bandwidth to properly vet candidates while keeping the business running. She's not lazy or unqualified at her job — she's just out of hours in the day, and out of network for this specific kind of hire.

**The turning point trigger:**
The gap reaches a point where it's actively costing the business — a patient wait time stretching out, an AI/Data Centre project stalling, or simply realizing she's spent weeks searching with nothing to show for it.

**Their internal monologue:**
"I don't have time to keep doing this myself. I've tried searching online but I don't even know if I'm looking for the right skillset anymore — the market's moved. I need someone who actually understands this space, not just another agency sending me random resumes. I want this filled with the right person, not just any person."

---

## ONE PROBLEM: THE VILLAIN

**External:** An open headcount that isn't getting filled fast enough, with no internal bandwidth or external network to fix it.

**Internal:** The growing sense of falling behind — both on the urgent hire and on understanding where the industry is actually heading.

**Philosophical:** Hiring managers shouldn't have to choose between filling a role quickly and filling it with someone who's actually right for where the business is going.

**The Villain Statement:**
"The real problem in the Stretched Hiring Manager's story is the gap between urgency and insight, which shows up as an unfilled role draining time and resources, makes them feel like they're falling behind both their hiring needs and the market itself, and represents the unfair truth that doing this hire well shouldn't require more time and expertise than they have to give."

---

## ONE SOLUTION: 3-STEP PLAN

**Step 1 — The Real Brief:**
Victor sits down with the hiring manager to understand not just the job description, but the purpose of the hire and the challenges they've faced so far. This shifts the conversation from "fill this seat" to "solve this business problem."

**Step 2 — AI-Assisted Screening:**
Rather than relying solely on a static database, Victor combines his proactively-collected candidate pool with AI tools to screen for the right skillset fast — sifting through what would otherwise be a manual, needle-in-a-haystack search.

**Step 3 — The Win-Win Close:**
Only qualified, screened candidates are sent forward. When the right person is found, Victor negotiates a placement that respects both the hiring manager's budget and the candidate's expectations — not just closing the deal for JOT's sake.

---

## ONE OUTCOME

**Tangible:** A qualified candidate placed in the role, backed by a 3-month replacement guarantee.

**Emotional:** Relief from the manual search, and confidence that the hire will actually stick.

**Identity:** A hiring manager who is seen — by their own leadership — as someone who reads the market well and makes informed decisions, not just reactive ones.

**Outcome Statement:**
"After working with Jobs of Tomorrow, the Stretched Hiring Manager will have the role filled with someone who genuinely fits, feel relief from no longer carrying the search alone, and see themselves as someone who hires with market insight, not just urgency."

The story of helping his friend's F&B business find an operations manager — which freed her to launch an entirely new business venture — is the clearest evidence of what he means when he says recruitment isn't just about filling gaps. It's what makes him a guide hiring managers can trust with more than just a job description.

## 7-PART BRAND STORY

### 1. A Character
She's the HR manager who's been juggling an open headcount for weeks now, squeezed between keeping daily operations running and trying to source candidates she barely has time to properly vet. Every job board search turns up resumes that look fine on paper but don't quite match what the role actually needs — and she's starting to wonder if she's even looking for the right things anymore.

### 2. Has a Problem
The role needs filling, but she doesn't have the network or the hours to do it properly. If it's a healthcare hire, there's a maze of foreign-worker regulations she's never had to navigate before. If it's an AI/Data Centre role, she's not sure the skillsets she's screening for are even the ones that matter anymore. The longer it stays open, the more it costs — in workload, in stalled projects, in her own confidence that she's making the right call.

### 3. Meets a Guide
Victor understands this isn't just a staffing gap — it's a business problem with real downstream impact. Rather than asking for a job description and disappearing, he sits down to understand the purpose behind the hire and what's already been tried. His database is refreshed daily using AI, and unlike agencies that take a cut from candidates' pay, JOT charges only the hiring company — a stance he's explicit about because he believes the alternative isn't right.

### 4. Who Gives Them a Plan
First, Victor scopes the real brief — the role, the purpose, the challenges already faced. Then he runs the requirement through his AI-assisted screening against a candidate pool that's updated daily, narrowing a wide field down to a genuinely qualified shortlist. From there, he negotiates the placement to work for both sides — her budget, the candidate's expectations — so the hire isn't a compromise dressed up as a win.

### 5. And Calls Them to Action
**Direct CTA:** Send Victor the role you're struggling to fill — he'll come back with a shortlist, not just a job description recap.
**Transitional CTA:** Not ready to hand off a search yet? Ask Victor what he's seeing in your industry's hiring trends right now.

### 6. That Helps Them Avoid Failure
Without the right partner, she keeps absorbing the cost of an open role — projects stalling, her team stretched thinner, and the nagging sense that she's hiring reactively instead of strategically. Worse, if she goes with an agency that's purely transactional, she risks a placement that doesn't stick, putting her right back at square one in a few months. And if she's hiring for AI/Data Centre talent with outdated assumptions about what skills matter, she risks the company falling behind competitors who are reading the market correctly.

### 7. And Ends in Success
Like the F&B founder in Victor's own story, she finally has the right person in the seat — and for the first time in weeks, she's not the one carrying the search. The role isn't just filled; it's filled with someone who fits, backed by a guarantee that gives her room to breathe. With the operational weight lifted, she has the bandwidth to focus on what's next for the business, instead of being stuck managing the gap that got her here.
