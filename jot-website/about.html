Recruitment as a read on where your industry is going.

Jobs of Tomorrow exists because hiring managers in healthcare and AI/Data Centre are stretched too thin to find the right talent on their own — and the agencies meant to help too often treat a hire as a box to check.

Who We Are

A market-informed consultancy, not a resume broker.

We believe a placement only counts as a win if it works for both the hiring company's budget and the candidate's expectations. We also believe that charging candidates fees — a stereotype that has long haunted this industry — is simply not right, and we refuse to do it.

Unlike agencies relying on static databases and outdated skillset assumptions, we combine a daily-updated, proactively-collected candidate pool with AI-assisted screening to move fast without sacrificing fit. AI speeds up the work; it does not replace the consultant's judgment on whether someone is genuinely right for the role.

That makes us a partner for hiring managers who need more than a resume — they need someone who understands the business impact of the hire, and negotiates as an extended arm of their team.

What We Stand For

Precise. Informed. Fair.

01

Lead with the market, not the vacancy

Every search starts with a read on where your industry and competitors are heading — because the right hire is the one that fits where you are going, not only where you are now.

02

A win-win, or it is not a placement

We factor in your budget and the candidate's expectations before closing. A deal that only serves one side is not a result we are interested in.

03

Never charge candidates

Our fees come from hiring companies only. Candidates are never charged — for anything, at any stage.

04

Fresh data over stale files

Our candidate database is refreshed daily, so the people we present are active and reachable — not leads that went cold months ago.

05

Transparent, even when it is inconvenient

If a search is not going well, we say so — and suggest how to fine-tune the role or the salary rather than pushing a poor fit through.

A Placement in Practice

The hire that freed a founder to build her next venture.

The founder of an F&B group needed an operations manager, but was too tied up in day-to-day operations to source one herself — and that gap was preventing her from expanding the business.

We scoped her requirements, searched our database with AI-assisted screening to speed up the process, and sent three to five qualified candidates within a couple of days. One was shortlisted and joined the company. Within a few months, the founder had handed over operational duties — and went on to successfully launch a second F&B brand.

That is how we measure a placement: not by the seat being filled, but by what the hire frees your business to do next.
